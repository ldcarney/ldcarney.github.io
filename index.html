Experience and Skills

National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)

I began working at NOAA as a Student Software Developer in December 2019 and have since become a temporary Software Developer at NOAA upon graduating in December 2020. As part of the Agile based Mulibeam Sonar Data Team I have been hands on in the redesign and implementation of a new data ingestion pipeline in accordance with Project Seabed 2030. Project Seabed 2030 is a mission to have the global seafloor completely mapped through the use of sonar technology by the year 2030. While working remotely, I was solely responsible for the reformatting and migration of data from the legacy database schema to a newly designed schema with completely new dataset structures. Through this task I have become very fluent in Python Dictionaries, SQL Statements, and Database Connection.

Boulder After-School Chess

From November 2018 to January 2020, I worked as an after school chess instructor at several Elementary Schools in Boulder County. I would hold hour long lessons to 20-30 elementary school students of mixed grades and backgrounds. Through this position I was able to learn how to communicate difficult concepts at a level in which my audience could comprehend. It has directly influenced the way I communicate advanced Computer Science topics to non-technical listeners.

About

I was originally born and raised on the North Shore of Massachusetts in a city called Gloucester. I attended Gloucester High School and I spent my summers lifeguarding, surfing and sailing along the beautiful coast of New England. Upon graduating high school I attended the University of Colorado Boulder as an undecided major for its excellent education and beautiful location in which I could ski, camp, flyfish and hike in my own backyard.

While the only experience I had in programming prior to attending CU Boulder was an Advanced Placement Computer Science course I took in high school, I decided to enroll in a few more programming classes in college to test the waters. I quickly found that the more I learned about computer science the more it would become a passion of mine. Where most people view computer science as a form of problem-solving, I view it as an ultimate form of creating. The possibilties that evolve from this form of science are endless which is what has driven me to pursue a career out of it.

In addition to coding, I find most of my enjoyment in the outdoors. Growing up on the ocean I spent most of my free time surfing, sailing, and fishing. Here in Colorado, I spend my time backcountry snowboarding, hiking, camping, and fly fishing. I value a balance between the time I spend on my computer and the time I spend outdoors as I understand the importance of keeping your body active as well as your mind.
